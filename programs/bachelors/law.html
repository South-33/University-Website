Core Objectives

  • 1

    Establish a strong foundation in legal theory, Cambodian law, and comparative legal systems.

  • 2

    Develop legal analysis, research, and advocacy skills through writing, moot courts, and clinics.

  • 3

    Prepare graduates for ethical legal practice, public service, and leadership in Cambodia and the region.

Why Law at NUM?

Experienced Faculty

Learn from judges, prosecutors, seasoned attorneys, and legal scholars.

Practice-Oriented

Moot court, legal aid clinics, internships with courts, firms, NGOs, and ministries.

Career-Focused

Aligned with legal market needs in litigation, corporate counsel, compliance, and public service.

Program Curriculum

Year 1

Foundations of Law

Semester I

  • Introduction to Political Science (IPS)
  • Legal Principles & Institutions (LPI)
  • Introduction to Law (INL)
  • Khmer Culture & History (KHCH)
  • English for Law I (ENL)

Semester II

  • ASEAN Legal Systems (ALS)
  • Criminology (CRG)
  • Administrative Law (ADL)
  • English for Law II (ENL II)
  • Environmental Law (EVL)

Year 2

Core Public and Private Law

Semester III

  • Constitutional Law (CSL)
  • Criminal Law I (CRL)
  • Civil Law I (CL)
  • Commercial Law I (CML)
  • Legal Writing (LEW)

Semester IV

  • Human Rights Law (HRL)
  • Criminal Law II (CRL II)
  • Civil Law II (CL II)
  • Commercial Law II (CML II)
  • Alternative Dispute Resolutions (ADR)

Year 3

Procedures and Practice

Semester V

  • Criminal Procedure (CRP)
  • Land Law (Property Law) (LAL)
  • Company & Bankruptcy Law (CBL)
  • Advanced Special Contract Law (ASC)

Semester VI

  • Private International Law (PIL)
  • Trial Advocacy Skills (TAS)
  • Family Law and Succession (FAL)
  • Commercial Transactions (COT)
  • Critical Legal Thinking and Writing (CLTW)

Year 4

Advanced Topics and Research

Semester VII

  • Labor Law (LAL)
  • Banking Law & Security Market (BLSM)
  • Public Finance (PUL)
  • Public International Law (PUL)
  • Legal Research Methods (LRM)

Semester VIII

  • Comparative Constitutional Law (CCL)
  • Comparative Administrative Law & Procedure (CA)
  • Comparative Criminal Law & Procedure (CCRP)
  • Comparative Civil Law & Procedure (CCLP)
  • Public Policy Analysis (PPA)

Career Opportunities

Pursue diverse legal careers across private practice, corporate, government, and civil society.

LEGAL PRACTICE

Lawyer (Attorney)

Advise and represent clients in civil, criminal, or commercial matters in court and arbitration.

IN-HOUSE

Corporate Counsel

Advise companies on contracts, governance, compliance, labor, and transactions.

JUDICIARY

Judge's Clerk / Prosecutor

Support judicial decision-making, draft opinions, and prosecute criminal cases.

GOVERNANCE

Compliance Officer

Ensure regulatory compliance in banking, securities, environment, and labor.

PUBLIC POLICY

Policy Analyst

Research and recommend legal and regulatory reforms for public interest.

ACADEMIA

Legal Researcher

Produce scholarship and contribute to legal reforms with think tanks and universities.
