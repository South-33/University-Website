About Our University

The National University of Management, located in Cambodia, is a modern educational institution with a history of 31 years, having progressed in capacity, diversity, and quality education, emphasizing research, entrepreneurship, and innovation. It is ranked 69th in the World University Rankings for Innovation (WURI) 2025, reflecting its commitment to developing competent, socially responsible individuals who contribute to the country's socio-economic growth.

The National University of Management was established for the public benefit and it is recognized nationally. Throughout our great history, the National University of Management has offered access to a wide range of academic opportunities.

OUR JOURNEY

The Evolution Of The National University Of Management

1983 – 1989

The institution was founded in 1983 as the Economic Institute. It was renamed the Institute of Economic Science (IES) in 1989, providing major fields in economics and commerce.

1990 – 1998

In 1994, IES became the Faculty of Business (FOB), launching programs in management, marketing, accounting, and finance. By 1998, it was renamed the National Institute of Management (NIM).

1999 – 2003

During this period, NIM expanded its curriculum and began offering private tuition programs, solidifying its reputation as a premier institution for business and management education.

2004 – Present

The institution transitioned into the National University of Management (NUM) in 2004 and became a public non-profit university in 2005, now offering a full range of bachelor's, master's, and doctoral programs.
